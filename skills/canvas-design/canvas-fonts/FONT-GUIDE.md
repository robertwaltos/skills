## Detailed Font Profiles

### Display & Headline Fonts

#### Boldonse
**Character**: Unapologetic boldness with geometric construction
**Personality**: Confident, modern, statement-making
**Pairs with**: CrimsonPro (contrast), WorkSans (harmony)
**Use when**: The design needs to command attention immediately
**Avoid when**: Subtlety or elegance is the goal

#### EricaOne
**Character**: Artistic weight with expressive curves
**Personality**: Creative, bold, artistic
**Pairs with**: Lora, InstrumentSans
**Use when**: Creating artistic posters or creative expressions
**Avoid when**: Professional/corporate contexts

#### YoungSerif
**Character**: Contemporary interpretation of classic serifs
**Personality**: Sophisticated, modern-classic, editorial
**Pairs with**: WorkSans, GeistMono
**Use when**: Editorial design, sophisticated branding
**Avoid when**: Playful or casual contexts

#### Gloock
**Character**: High-contrast display with dramatic thick-thin strokes
**Personality**: Fashion-forward, luxurious, editorial
**Pairs with**: InstrumentSans, DMMono
**Use when**: Fashion, luxury brands, high-end editorial
**Avoid when**: Technical or casual content

#### Italiana
**Character**: Art Deco elegance with graceful proportions
**Personality**: Refined, timeless, upscale
**Pairs with**: Lora, CrimsonPro
**Use when**: Luxury branding, elegant invitations, refined aesthetics
**Avoid when**: Modern tech or casual contexts

#### PoiretOne
**Character**: Geometric Art Deco with circular forms
**Personality**: Vintage-modern, geometric elegance
**Pairs with**: WorkSans, Outfit
**Use when**: 1920s-inspired design, geometric compositions
**Avoid when**: Heavy body text needs

## Typography Best Practices for Canvas Design

### Hierarchy Principles

1. **Establish clear levels**: Maximum 3 typographic levels per composition
2. **Size contrast**: Headlines should be 2-3x body size minimum for impact
3. **Weight contrast**: Combine light and bold weights for visual interest
4. **Spacing**: Increase letter-spacing for large headlines, decrease for body text

### Pairing Rules

1. **Contrast principle**: Pair serif with sans-serif, or geometric with humanist
2. **Era consistency**: Fonts from similar eras harmonize better
3. **X-height matching**: Pair fonts with similar x-heights for body text
4. **Limit variety**: Maximum 2-3 fonts per composition

### Font Selection Decision Tree

```
Is the design primarily:
├── Editorial/Reading → Lora, CrimsonPro, LibreBaskerville
├── Technical/Data → JetBrainsMono, GeistMono, InstrumentSans
├── Artistic/Expressive → EricaOne, Boldonse, Gloock
├── Corporate/Professional → WorkSans, Outfit, IBMPlexMono
├── Luxury/Refined → Italiana, YoungSerif, Gloock
└── Playful/Casual → SmoochSans, PixelifySans, NothingYouCouldDo
```

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Using decorative fonts for body text
❌ Combining more than 3 fonts in one composition
❌ Ignoring x-height when pairing fonts
❌ Using condensed fonts for long reading
❌ Overusing novelty/display fonts
❌ Neglecting line-height (leading) adjustments
