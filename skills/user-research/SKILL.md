## Core Philosophy

**Research is not validationâ€”it's discovery.** Every research decision should:

1. **Reduce Risk**: Validate assumptions before expensive development
2. **Build Empathy**: Create genuine understanding of user needs
3. **Drive Action**: Produce insights that change decisions
4. **Be Rigorous**: Use appropriate methods with proper sample sizes
5. **Be Timely**: Deliver insights when they can influence outcomes

## User Interviews

### Interview Guide Structure

```markdown
# Interview Guide: [Topic]

## Session Information
- **Duration**: 60 minutes
- **Format**: Remote video call / In-person
- **Recording**: Yes (with consent)
- **Incentive**: [$X gift card]

---

## Introduction (5 minutes)

### Welcome Script
"Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. My name is [Name], and I'm a researcher at [Company]. We're working to understand [general topic] to improve our [product/service].

Before we begin:
- This conversation will take about 60 minutes
- There are no right or wrong answersâ€”I'm here to learn from your experience
- With your permission, I'd like to record this session for note-taking purposes. The recording will only be used internally and will be deleted after analysis
- You can skip any question or stop at any time
- Do you have any questions before we start?"

### Consent
"Do I have your permission to record this session?"

---

## Warm-up (5 minutes)

### Context Questions
1. "Tell me a little about yourself and your role."
2. "How long have you been [doing activity related to research topic]?"
3. "Walk me through a typical day/week when you [activity]."

*Goal: Build rapport, establish context, ease into the conversation*

---

## Core Questions (40 minutes)

### Topic 1: Current Behavior (15 min)
1. "Tell me about the last time you [did relevant activity]. Walk me through what happened."
   - Probe: "What happened next?"
   - Probe: "How did that make you feel?"
   - Probe: "What tools/resources did you use?"

2. "What's the hardest part about [activity]?"
   - Probe: "Can you give me a specific example?"
   - Probe: "How do you currently handle that?"

3. "What does a successful [outcome] look like for you?"

### Topic 2: Pain Points & Needs (15 min)
4. "What frustrates you most about [current process/tools]?"
   - Probe: "How often does this happen?"
   - Probe: "What's the impact when it does?"

5. "If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about [topic], what would it be?"

6. "How do you currently work around [specific problem identified]?"

### Topic 3: Specific Concept Exploration (10 min)
7. [Show concept/prototype if applicable]
   "I'd like to show you something we're exploring. This is early work, so please share your honest reactions."
   
   - "What's your initial reaction?"
   - "What questions do you have?"
   - "How might this fit into your [workflow/life]?"
   - "What concerns would you have about using this?"

---

## Wrap-up (10 minutes)

### Summary Questions
8. "Of everything we discussed, what's most important to you?"

9. "Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?"

10. "Is there anyone else you think I should talk to about this topic?"

### Closing Script
"Thank you so much for your time and insights today. Your feedback is incredibly valuable and will directly influence [what we're building/how we improve]. 

Your incentive will be sent to [email] within [timeframe]. If you have any questions or think of anything else, please don't hesitate to reach out."

```

### Interview Best Practices

**DO:**
- Ask open-ended questions ("Tell me about..." vs "Do you...")
- Use silenceâ€”let participants fill the space
- Follow interesting threads even if off-script
- Ask for specific examples and stories
- Probe with "Why?" and "Tell me more"
- Take notes on emotions and body language

**DON'T:**
- Ask leading questions ("Don't you think...?")
- Stack multiple questions together
- Fill silence too quickly
- Suggest answers
- Show agreement/disagreement with opinions
- Talk more than 20% of the time

### Think-Aloud Protocol

**Concurrent Think-Aloud (during task):**
"As you work through this, please think out loudâ€”tell me what you're looking at, what you're thinking, what you're trying to do, even if it seems obvious."

**Reminders during session:**
- "What are you thinking right now?"
- "What are you looking for?"
- "What do you expect to happen?"

**Retrospective Think-Aloud (after task):**
Play back screen recording and ask participant to narrate their thought process.

### Survey Best Practices

**Question Design:**
- Keep questions short and simple
- One concept per question (no double-barreled questions)
- Use consistent scales throughout
- Include "Not applicable" or "I don't know" when relevant
- Randomize option order for non-ordered lists

**Response Options:**
- 5-point scale for quick surveys, 7-point for more granularity
- Always label endpoints, optionally label midpoint
- Balance positive and negative options
- Avoid "somewhat agree" ambiguityâ€”use frequency or intensity

**Survey Flow:**
- Start with easy, engaging questions
- Put sensitive questions later (after trust is built)
- Group related questions together
- Required questions sparingly (especially open-ended)
- Show progress indicator for longer surveys

### Affinity Mapping Process

```markdown
## Affinity Mapping Steps

1. **Gather Data**
   - Export all notes, quotes, observations
   - One insight per sticky note (physical or Miro/FigJam)
   - Include participant ID for traceability

2. **Initial Clustering**
   - Silently group similar items
   - Don't name groups yet
   - Multiple researchers reduce bias

3. **Refine Groups**
   - Discuss and adjust groupings
   - Split large groups (>20 items)
   - Combine small groups (<3 items)

4. **Name Themes**
   - Create descriptive theme names
   - Themes should be insights, not topics
   - Bad: "Onboarding"
   - Good: "Users feel overwhelmed by initial setup complexity"

5. **Identify Hierarchies**
   - Group themes into higher-level categories
   - Create 4-8 major themes

6. **Prioritize**
   - Mark themes by frequency (how many mentioned)
   - Mark themes by intensity (strength of feeling)
   - Identify cross-cutting themes
```

### Jobs-to-be-Done Framework

```markdown
## JTBD Interview Structure

### Core Question Format
"When _______ [situation], I want to _______ [motivation], so I can _______ [expected outcome]."

### Discovery Questions

### Forces Analysis
| Force | Question to Ask |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Push** (away from current) | "What wasn't working about your old way?" |
| **Pull** (toward new) | "What attracted you to this solution?" |
| **Anxiety** (fear of new) | "What concerns did you have about switching?" |
| **Habit** (comfort with old) | "What did you like about how you used to do it?" |

- Example: "Organize my financial documents for tax season"

**Related Jobs**:
- [Associated job 1]
- [Associated job 2]

**Emotional Jobs**:
- Feel [emotion] about [aspect]
- Avoid feeling [negative emotion]

**Social Jobs**:
- Be seen as [perception]
- Belong to [group]
```

## Quality Checklist

### Before Research
- [ ] Research questions are specific and answerable
- [ ] Method matches the questions
- [ ] Sample size is appropriate for method
- [ ] Recruitment criteria are clear
- [ ] Guide/script has been piloted
- [ ] Stakeholders aligned on objectives

### During Research
- [ ] Consent obtained and documented
- [ ] Recording working (if applicable)
- [ ] Following guide but allowing natural conversation
- [ ] Taking notes on non-verbal cues
- [ ] Not leading or biasing participants
- [ ] Debriefing after each session

### After Research
- [ ] Analysis method appropriate for data type
- [ ] Multiple researchers involved in synthesis
- [ ] Findings tied to evidence
- [ ] Recommendations are actionable
- [ ] Report reviewed by stakeholders
- [ ] Insights added to repository

---

## Final Step: Impact Measurement

Research is only successful if it influences decisions. After each project:

1. **Track Decisions**: Document what decisions were made based on findings
2. **Measure Outcomes**: Follow up on implemented recommendations
3. **Build Credibility**: Share impact stories with stakeholders
4. **Improve Process**: Retrospect on what worked and what didn't

The research is complete when insights have been communicated, decisions have been influenced, and the knowledge is preserved for future reference.
